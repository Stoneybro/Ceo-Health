Ultrasound

Ultrasound is a non-invasive medical imaging technique that uses sound waves to produce images of the organs and structures inside your body. It is a safe and painless procedure that can help your doctor diagnose various medical conditions such as gall bladder disease, breast lumps, genital and prostate issues, joint inflammation, blood flow problems, and monitoring pregnancy. During the procedure, a technician will apply gel to your skin and use a small probe to capture images of your internal organs and tissues. Ultrasound imaging is an excellent diagnostic tool because it is non-invasive, does not require exposure to ionizing radiation, and can produce high-resolution images of soft tissues that may not be visible on x-rays. The procedure typically lasts between 30 minutes to an hour, depending on the specific area being imaged. If you are experiencing any medical issues or symptoms, an ultrasound can provide your doctor with valuable information to make an accurate diagnosis and create an effective treatment plan. CT-Scan

Our CT-Scan service utilizes the latest imaging technology to produce detailed cross-sectional images of your body's internal structures. Our state-of-the-art equipment provides our team of experienced radiologists with precise information to help diagnose a variety of medical conditions. With CT-Scan, we can accurately identify bone fractures, injuries from trauma, tumors and cancers, vascular and heart disease, and infections. Additionally, CT-Scan is an excellent tool to guide biopsies and ensure their accuracy. During the procedure, you will be comfortably positioned on a table that slides into the scanner, which looks like a large doughnut. Our team will ensure that you are comfortable and provide you with clear instructions on how to remain still during the imaging process. The x-ray tube rotates around you to capture detailed images, and the procedure usually takes only 10-15 minutes. MRI

At UVA Radiology and Medical Imaging, we offer an MRI service that utilizes magnetic fields and radio waves to create highly detailed images of organs and tissues in the body. Our advanced imaging technology allows our experienced radiologists to diagnose a variety of conditions, including multiple sclerosis (MS), stroke, spinal cord disorders, tumors, blood vessel issues, joint or tendon injuries, and more. When you arrive for your MRI, our friendly staff will guide you through the process and answer any questions you may have. You will lie on a table that slides into the MRI machine, which is deeper and narrower than a CT scanner.While the MRI magnets are in use, you may hear loud tapping and thumping noises, but rest assured that the procedure is completely safe and non-invasive. The MRI typically lasts between 45 minutes to an hour, during which time our advanced imaging technology will generate detailed images of your body's internal structures. PET Scan

PET scans are a non-invasive imaging technique that uses radioactive tracers to detect and diagnose various medical conditions. This diagnostic procedure is used to determine the functionality of various organs and tissues in the body. The PET scanner is a machine that detects the radiation produced by the radiotracer, and produces detailed images of the area being examined. During a PET scan, the patient is given a radiotracer either through injection or ingestion, which will travel to the specific area being examined. Once the radiotracer has been absorbed, the patient will enter the scanner, which will produce detailed images of the body. The entire process can take up to 2 hours, depending on the area being examined. PET scans are commonly used to diagnose cancer, heart disease, Alzheimer's disease, seizures, epilepsy, and Parkinson's disease. These scans can also be used to identify any changes in the body's metabolism, which can be indicative of various medical conditions.
